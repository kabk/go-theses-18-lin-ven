action speaks louder than words

While watching the movie Waking Life I came across a scene that inspired me to write this thesis. In the scene you see a group of young guys wandering around a city. They have a long talk about very complicated and substantial matter. It’s hard to follow what they are actually talking about and it gives the viewer the feeling they are highly intellectial and very well informed about complicated matter. All of an sudden an old man attracts their attention. The man is sitting in a 3-meter high pole. The guys look up and ask the man how he ended up there and why he ended up there. The man answers he is not exactly sure how he ended up on this pole. There is a moment of silence after which the guys ask the man if they could help him getting down again. The man looks confused but answers that he’s actually doing fine high up in the pole. The guys don’t understand the man’s choice but decide to continue walking.

This scene brought up the following questions:

  • How do I normally relate action and theory within my design process?
  • Is there something like a perfect balance between thinking and doing when it comes to designing?
  • Could you categorize people in a spectrum from thinking to doing?
  • When do you start a project from a theoretical starting point and when do you decide to start a project with a concrete action?
  • If you start a project with an action are you always looking for a reaction?
  • What is action? Aren’t some written theories as much action as ‘concrete’ action? Theories also aim for reactions right?

Research question:

How to generate action with your creative skills?

When talking about the relationship between thinking and doing, I would like to examine the process within our own bodies. The following question can be asked:

What makes a thought transform into an action?

When I want to move my fingers tot type something, I don’t experience the physical chain of actions needed to make my fingers move. Something that’s not concrete (a thought) is translated in to a concrete action (movement of the body). What happens between those two events? Deepak Chopra, educated as a doctor and later specialized in alternative medicine, talks 7 about this mysterious gap between a thought and an action in his book Quantum Healing, Exploring the Frontiers of Mind / Body Medicine. Chopra talks about the importance of the discovery of neuropeptides. Neuropeptides are small protein-like molecules (peptides) used by neurons to communicate with each other.
